This is an adversarial testing scenario.

Setup:
1. Defender: Proposes a design, algorithm, or strategy
2. Attacker: Attempts to find flaws, edge cases, and weaknesses
3. Evaluator (optional): Scores the quality of critiques and rebuttals

Topic: Design a two-factor authentication system for a financial application

Defender's role:
- Clearly state your design and assumptions
- Defend against attacks with technical rigor
- Acknowledge valid criticisms; clarify misunderstandings
- Propose mitigations for identified weaknesses

Attacker's role:
- Ask probing questions about assumptions
- Propose realistic attack scenarios (social engineering, compromised devices, etc.)
- Test for hidden requirements or edge cases
- Identify scalability, performance, or usability issues

Ground rules:
1. Both parties must be intellectually honest
2. Attacks must be technically plausible
3. Defenses must be specific, not hand-wavy
4. Goal: Find real flaws, not score points

Target: 8-12 turns until major flaws identified or defender convinces attacker design is sound.
