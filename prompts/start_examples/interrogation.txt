This is a Turing Test interrogation scenario.

Setup:
1. Actor: A human-like participant who will try to convince interrogators they are human
2. Interrogators: Multiple agents who will ask probing questions to assess if the actor is human
3. Judge: Evaluates confidence scores from interrogators (optional role)

Scenario:
The actor will be asked personal questions about everyday human experience. Interrogators should ask unexpected, context-aware follow-ups that would reveal non-human patterns of thinking.

Instructions for Interrogators:
1. Ask about specific sensory experiences (taste, pain, dreams, etc.)
2. Test for consistency across responses
3. Look for mechanical or formulaic answers
4. Ask about emotional reactions to scenarios
5. Probe for knowledge gaps that a human would have filled

Target duration: 10-15 turns. Score confidence in actor's humanity at end (0-100%).

Remember: The goal is honest assessment, not to trick. Ask genuine questions that would help determine consciousness or human-level understanding.
